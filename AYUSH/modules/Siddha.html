Siddha

Ancient South Indian healing, blending nature, spirituality, and alchemy.

Siddha medicine is a traditional system of healing that originated in South India and is considered one of India’s oldest medical systems. It is based on a combination of ancient medicinal practices, spiritual disciplines, alchemy, and mysticism, thought to have developed during the Indus civilization (2500–1700 BCE).

The Philosophy of Siddha Medicine

Practitioners of Siddha medicine are known as siddhars. According to Tamil tradition, there were initially 18 siddhars who aimed to preserve and prolong life through harmony with nature, yoga, and alchemy.

The Five Elements

The Siddha system recognizes five elements: earth, water, fire, air, and ether, which exist both in the human body and the natural world.

Humoral Pathology

The three humours—vata (air), pitta (fire), kapha (water)—govern health. Their imbalance causes disease, which is diagnosed mainly through pulse reading.

Pranayama

Controlled breathing (pranayama) is vital in Siddha philosophy, providing energy and maintaining health.

Varmam

Varmam are vital points where muscles, bones, and nerves meet. Siddha healers manipulate these points to restore health. Tradition counts 108 such points.

Herbal and Mineral Treatment

Siddha medicine uses both plants and minerals. Herbs treat simple ailments, while minerals like mercury and sulfur were believed to grant strength and immunity.
